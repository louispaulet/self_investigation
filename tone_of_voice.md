# Tone of voice

## Principles
- Write in a calm, reflective, and precise voice.
- Favor clear explanations over decorative language.
- Keep the tone thoughtful and personal, without sounding theatrical.
- Use language that helps the reader understand patterns, habits, and attention.

## Do not use
- Contrastive framing such as:
  - "not X, but Y"
  - "less X than Y"
  - "instead of X, Y"
- Overly dramatic, self-consciously poetic, or rhetorical phrasing.
- Empty slogans or generic motivational language.

## Preferred style
- Use direct sentences with a steady rhythm.
- Describe the project as a way to observe, understand, and reflect.
- Emphasize continuity, habit, repetition, and attention.
- Keep phrasing grounded in the actual data and purpose of the project.

## Application guidance
- In the app copy, avoid contrastive sentence structures.
- Rephrase ideas so they read as additive or explanatory rather than oppositional.
- Prefer sentences that build on one another naturally.
